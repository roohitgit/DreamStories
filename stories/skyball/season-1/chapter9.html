Chapter 9: Tiranga Beijing Me Lehraya

Final buzzer ke baad bhi stadium ka noise ruk nahi raha tha.

“INDIA! INDIA! INDIA!”

Pure Sky Dome me sirf ek hi naam gunj raha tha.

Indian players ek dusre ko hug kar rahe the. Kuch players ground par baith kar ro rahe the.

Vikram ne Rohit ko pakad kar utha liya.

“Ye teri jeet hai bhai!”

Rohit haste hue bola,

“Nahi… ye India ki jeet hai.”

Udhar Brazil ke captain Rafael bhi Indian team ke paas aaya.

Usne Vikram aur Rohit se handshake kiya.

“You deserved this.”

Ye words sunte hi Indian fans aur emotional ho gaye.

Presentation ceremony start hone wali thi.

Stage ke center me World Skyball Trophy rakhi thi.

Golden silver metal se bani hui giant floating sphere.

Uske neeche ek line likhi thi.

“Victory Belongs To The Fearless.”

Commentator ki heavy voice stadium me gunji.

“After years of heartbreak… India has finally become World Champion.”

Indian national anthem start hua.

Jaise hi Tiranga upar uthne laga…

Pure Indian section me log ro pade.

Players bhi anthem gaate hue emotional ho gaye.

Coach Arvind ke aansu ruk nahi rahe the.

Unhone Rohit ki taraf dekha.

“Tu samajhta hai tune kya kar diya?”

Rohit quietly Tirange ko dekh raha tha.

“Bas sach saamne laaya sir.”

Tabhi stadium ki giant screen suddenly ON hui.

International Federation Chairperson Elena live announcement dene wali thi.

“After complete investigation…”

Pure stadium me silence.

“China has officially been stripped of all previous World Skyball titles won using illegal equipment.”

Crowd shocked.

Chinese fans bhi chup ho gaye.

Elena ne continue kiya,

“All involved officials are permanently banned from international sports.”

Pure sports world me earthquake aa gaya.

India officially 3 past tournaments ka rightful finalist declare hua.

Indian crowd aur zor se cheer karne laga.

Par sabse emotional moment abhi baaki tha.

Federation ne Rohit ko stage par bulaya.

“Mr. Rohit Badole.”

Pure stadium me standing ovation shuru ho gaya.

Rohit awkward smile ke sath stage par gaya.

Elena ne usse special medal diya.

“This medal is awarded for protecting the spirit of world sports.”

Rohit ne medal liya.

Uske dimaag me woh pehla din flash hua jab players uska mazaak uda rahe the.

28 saal ka chota analyst.

Aur aaj…

Wahi ladka world sports history ka hero ban gaya tha.

Media reporters continuously photos le rahe the.

Ek reporter ne loudly poocha,

“Rohit! Aapko kaisa lag raha hai?”

Rohit kuch second chup raha.

Phir slowly bola,

“Jab desh ka naam chest par hota hai na… tab haar personal lagti hai.”

Pure stadium me silence.

“Aur jab jeet milti hai… to lagta hai poora desh saath jeeta hai.”

Massive applause.

Udhar India me subah ho chuki thi.

Sadko par celebration start ho gaya.

Mumbai. Delhi. Pune. Kolkata. Chennai.

Har jagah log dance kar rahe the.

Crackers phoot rahe the.

TV channels continuously ek hi headline dikha rahe the.

“INDIA WORLD CHAMPION”

Aur ek photo sab jagah viral ho chuki thi.

Rohit. Haath me Tiranga. Peeche scoreboard. INDIA WINNER.

Par ussi raat…

Beijing ke ek dark office me koi silently TV dekh raha tha.

Screen par Rohit ki speech chal rahi thi.

Us aadmi ne slowly TV OFF kiya.

Aur dheere se bola,

“This is not over…”

Room darkness me doob gaya.
