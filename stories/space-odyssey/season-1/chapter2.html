Chapter 2: Departure into the Unknown

The countdown had begun. The launchpad at the Satish Dhawan Space Centre in Sriharikota was alive with activity as the final preparations for ISRO’s most ambitious mission took place. The spacecraft, Vyom-1, stood tall, its sleek frame reflecting the glow of the floodlights. Powered by the latest near-light-speed propulsion technology, it was humanity’s first vessel designed to carry a crew beyond the Solar System.

RoHit sat in the mission control room, surrounded by his crewmates, each donning their flight suits. The air was thick with anticipation. In just a few hours, they would embark on a journey no human had ever taken before.

“Suit up,” Commander Vikram Patel ordered, his voice steady and authoritative. Despite the immense pressure, he carried himself with unwavering confidence.

RoHit grinned. “If I pass out mid-flight, just pretend I’m meditating.”

Dr. Aditi Rao chuckled. “Don’t worry, I’ll document it as a scientific phenomenon.”

One by one, the crew suited up, their minds racing with thoughts of what lay ahead. Dr. Ananya Iyer, the medical officer, double-checked her supplies, while Arjun Prasad, the chief engineer, reviewed system diagnostics. Samar Mehta, the AI expert, ran a final test on Vyom-1’s onboard systems.

The voice of mission control echoed through their helmets. “All systems are go. Initiating final countdown.”

As they made their way to the spacecraft, RoHit took a final look at the night sky. This was it the moment he had dreamed of since childhood. Yet, a part of him still felt the weight of doubt. He wasn’t the best at anything, but he was here. He had a role to play.

The crew entered Vyom-1, strapping themselves into their seats. The control panels flickered to life, displaying streams of data. The engines rumbled beneath them, a powerful reminder of the force that was about to propel them into the cosmos.

The countdown reached zero. A deafening roar filled the cabin as the engines ignited, pushing them upward with immense force. Earth’s gravity fought to hold them back, but Vyom-1 broke free, piercing the atmosphere and entering the vastness of space.

RoHit felt the shift as the artificial gravity stabilized. He looked out of the viewport, watching as Earth slowly shrank behind them. A strange mix of awe and melancholy settled over him. The journey into the unknown had officially begun.
