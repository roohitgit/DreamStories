Chapter 4: The Reactor Malfunction

Just as the crew began to settle into their routine aboard Vyom-1, an unexpected crisis emerged. The ship’s primary reactor, responsible for powering all critical systems, began fluctuating beyond safe parameters.

Dr. Ananya Iyer, the medical officer, was the first to notice the dimming lights. “Is it just me, or is the ship having mood swings?”

Arjun Prasad, already on alert, checked his console. “It’s the reactor. We have unstable energy output. If this continues, we might lose critical systems.”

Samar Mehta tapped into the ship’s AI. “Vyom, diagnose the issue.”

The AI’s voice responded calmly. “Reactor output irregular. Possible coolant system failure.”

RoHit clapped his hands together. “Great. So, in short, our spaceship is overheating like an old laptop.”

Vikram Patel shot him a look but couldn’t suppress a grin. “We need a solution fast. Arjun, what’s our best option?”

Arjun frowned. “We can manually bypass the coolant failure, but it’ll require someone to enter the reactor bay and adjust the flow valves directly.”

Aditi Rao looked at the readings. “The radiation levels are within safe limits for a short duration, but the access panel is in a zero-gravity zone. We need someone who can maneuver efficiently.”

RoHit cracked his knuckles. “Sounds like a job for me.”

Vikram raised an eyebrow. “You sure about this?”

RoHit gave a thumbs-up. “I may not be an engineer, but I’m very good at following dramatic instructions while floating heroically.”

Arjun quickly briefed him on the task. With a tether secured to his suit, RoHit glided into the reactor bay. Beads of sweat formed on his forehead as he reached the valve.

“Okay, step one: don’t break anything. Step two: definitely don’t break anything.”

Carefully, he adjusted the coolant flow, using Arjun’s instructions relayed through his earpiece. The system slowly stabilized. With one final turn of the valve, the reactor output normalized.

As he floated back, the lights brightened. The crew cheered.

RoHit bowed dramatically. “And that, my friends, is how you fix a spaceship - Bollywood style.”

The crisis averted, the crew marveled at how RoHit’s humor and quick thinking turned what could have been a disaster into a bonding moment. With Vyom-1 back on track, they set their sights on the next phase of their journey.
