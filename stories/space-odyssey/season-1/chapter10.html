Chapter 10: A New Dawn at Alpha Centauri

The journey had been grueling, filled with unforeseen dangers, cosmic mysteries, and moments where the crew had nearly lost all hope. Yet, as Vyom-1 decelerated near its destination, the vast expanse of the Alpha Centauri system lay before them - a sight no human had ever witnessed in person.

The entire cabin was silent, mesmerized by the view outside. Three bright suns painted the darkness with hues of gold and crimson. The closest exoplanet, their target, shimmered against the backdrop of the cosmos, its surface a mixture of rocky terrain and what appeared to be vast oceans.

RoHit, as usual, broke the silence. “Well, well, well! Looks like our cosmic Airbnb finally showed up. I just hope they have WiFi.”

Aditi smirked. “If they do, I’m blocking you first.”

Commander Vikram Patel chuckled but quickly returned to focus. “Alright, team. Let’s get to work. We have one chance to make history.”

Landing on the Unknown.

Arjun Prasad, the chief engineer, guided Vyom-1 into orbit while Samar analyzed the planet’s atmosphere. Dr. Ananya Iyer monitored the crew’s vitals, ensuring they were stable for descent.

“The atmosphere seems breathable,” Samar announced. “High oxygen levels, but we should keep our suits on until we confirm microbial safety.”

RoHit clapped. “Perfect! No need for space masks! But just in case, let’s not start sniffing alien flowers yet.”

The spacecraft gently descended, landing on a flat, rocky plateau near a body of water. The crew suited up and stepped onto the alien world. The ground was firm, yet slightly different from Earth’s soil. Gravity was slightly lower, making each step feel lighter.

Aditi looked around. “Can’t believe it… we’re standing on an exoplanet.”

RoHit spread his arms dramatically. “And to think, I was just planning to binge-watch shows back on Earth!”

First Steps into the Unknown.

Their first task was setting up a temporary base and deploying instruments to analyze the planet’s environment. As Vikram and Arjun set up the communication systems, Aditi and Ananya took samples of the soil and air. RoHit, however, was assigned to assist with deploying solar panels.

“Why am I always the errand boy?” RoHit grumbled playfully, adjusting the solar panels.

Aditi smirked. “Because someone has to make sure the lights stay on while the real scientists do their work.”

Just as RoHit was about to fire back with a witty remark, a strange sound echoed from a nearby ridge.

A Mysterious Discovery.

The crew froze. The sound was rhythmic, almost like a pattern of taps. Samar quickly checked the signal monitors, but nothing unusual showed up.

Vikram gestured. “Let’s proceed with caution. RoHit, you’re on point.”

RoHit sighed. “Of course! Send the funny guy into the creepy alien unknown.”

As they approached the ridge, they saw something that made their hearts race - a structure, partially buried in the ground. It was metallic but worn with time, suggesting an artificial origin.

Aditi’s jaw dropped. “This… this isn’t natural.”

RoHit knelt down, wiping dust off a strange marking on the surface. “I swear, if this is an ancient alien ‘No Parking’ sign, I’m gonna be so disappointed.”

Unlocking the Past.

Arjun and Samar worked quickly to analyze the structure. It seemed to be centuries, if not millennia, old. With careful precision, they managed to activate what looked like a control panel. A faint glow emitted from the surface.

Suddenly, a holographic projection appeared, showing what seemed to be an alien civilization. They saw images of towering cities, strange creatures, and a disaster that had wiped them out. The last image showed a distress signal being sent - directly toward Earth.

Ananya gasped. “They knew we existed?”

RoHit, for once, was silent. Then he spoke, voice uncharacteristically serious. “Maybe we weren’t the first travelers to make it this far… maybe we were just the first to survive.”

A Heroic Finale.

Before they could process the revelation, a massive tremor shook the ground. The ancient structure was collapsing!

Vikram shouted, “Everyone, fall back!”

As they ran, a massive chunk of debris began falling toward Aditi. Without thinking, RoHit lunged, pushing her out of the way just in time. The debris crashed behind them, sealing off the entrance to the structure forever.

Aditi, still catching her breath, looked at RoHit. “You… you saved me?”

RoHit grinned. “Well, I can’t have you taunting me if you’re crushed under alien ruins, can I?”

The crew gathered back at the landing site, hearts still racing. They had uncovered proof of extraterrestrial intelligence and survived an encounter with a planet that had once harbored life.

The Final Message.

As they transmitted their findings back to Earth, RoHit sat by the ridge, gazing at the alien sunset.

Vikram approached. “You did good today, RoHit.”

RoHit smirked. “I know, right? Heroic, charming, and still ridiculously good-looking.”

Vikram chuckled. “We made it, RoHit. We did what no human has ever done.”

RoHit nodded. “Yeah… but now, we’re also part of something bigger. A story that started long before us.”

As the twin suns of Alpha Centauri set, the crew of Vyom-1 knew one thing - they weren’t just explorers. They were pioneers of a new age.

Humanity had arrived among the stars, and they were here to stay.
