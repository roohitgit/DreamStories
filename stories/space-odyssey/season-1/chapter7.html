Chapter 7: The Cosmic Cipher

As Vyom-1 continued its voyage toward Alpha Centauri, the mysterious signal evolved. What once seemed like background interference had taken the form of a structured pulse, like a coded message waiting to be deciphered.

RoHit, floating lazily near the control panel, popped a snack into his mouth. “Alright, who ordered the cosmic Morse code? Because I swear, if it’s a telemarketer, I’m going outside to tell them off personally.”

Aditi, who was busy analyzing the signal, rolled her eyes. “RoHit, this is serious. If you had studied signal patterns instead of memorizing bad jokes, you’d know this is structured.”

RoHit smirked. “Hey, my jokes are fantastic. And structured, thank you very much.”

Commander Vikram Patel exhaled. “Either way, we need to approach carefully. Arjun, can we triangulate the source?”

Arjun tapped at his console, his expression darkening. “You’re not going to like this. The signal is coming from our trajectory - about 500,000 kilometers ahead.”

Silence settled over the cabin. Something - or someone - was out there.

RoHit clapped his hands together. “Fantastic. First alien encounter, and we didn’t even bring samosas for first contact. Great job, ISRO.”

Despite the tension, his humor lifted the crew’s spirits. But as they drew closer, their long-range cameras picked up a structure - massive, metallic, and unlike anything built by humans.

Aditi’s jaw dropped. “That… that’s not a natural formation.”

RoHit leaned in. “Okay. That’s new. And also mildly terrifying.”

As they approached, the signal grew stronger. Samar decoded another layer. “This… this is a response to our own transmissions. It’s been bouncing off something.”

RoHit grinned. “So, the real question is - who’s been listening?”

The crew stared at the alien structure floating in the void. Its surface shimmered, reflecting light in strange, shifting patterns. The signal pulsed stronger, rhythmic, almost… welcoming?

Aditi, still focused on her data, sighed. “We need a controlled approach. We don’t know if it’s safe.”

RoHit, arms crossed, nodded sagely. “Right. Which means I should definitely not touch anything.”

Vikram looked at him suspiciously. “Why do I feel like you’ve already done something?”

A sudden alarm blared.

Aditi’s head whipped around. “RoHit, what did you do?”

RoHit held up his hands. “Nothing! I just… leaned on this console… a little. Maybe pressed a button. But only because it was in my way!”

The alien structure began emitting a new, rapid sequence of signals. The ship vibrated slightly, and the main screen displayed shifting symbols.

Samar’s fingers flew over the console. “Uh, guys? The ship’s computer just got invited into some kind of handshake protocol. I think RoHit accidentally activated a communication link.”

Aditi groaned. “Of course he did.”

RoHit pointed at himself. “Hey! Maybe I was meant to do this. Ever think of that?”

The screen flickered, and suddenly, the vibrations stopped. A new message appeared - translated by the AI:

“Welcome, travelers. You are the first. We have waited.”

RoHit blinked. “Oh. Well, that’s not ominous at all.”

Vikram straightened. “They were expecting us?”

Aditi, stunned, nodded. “Looks like it.”

RoHit grinned. “See? Accidental hero. Again. Someone write this down.”
