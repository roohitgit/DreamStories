Chapter 8: The Unexpected Invitation

The alien message continued flashing across the screen, the strange symbols translating into comprehensible text:

“Welcome, travelers. You are the first. We have waited.”

RoHit, leaning against the console, whistled. “Well, that’s a dramatic way to say ‘hello.’”

Aditi crossed her arms. “RoHit, you accidentally pressed a button and made first contact with an alien intelligence. You accidentally changed the course of human history. Again.”

RoHit grinned. “And yet, no one is thanking me. I mean, come on, where’s my medal?”

Commander Vikram Patel cleared his throat. “Focus, people. We need to decide our next move.”

Samar Mehta, the communications expert, studied the transmission. “It looks like an invitation. They’ve been waiting for someone to respond.”

Arjun Prasad, the chief engineer, frowned. “Waiting… for how long?”

Samar adjusted a few settings. “If I’m reading this correctly… centuries.”

Aditi’s eyebrows shot up. “Centuries? How is that even possible?”

RoHit shrugged. “Maybe they just really like playing hard to get.”

The crew exchanged glances. This wasn’t just a random signal - this was an ancient attempt at communication, waiting for a response that had finally come.

Vikram took a deep breath. “Alright. We proceed with caution. Samar, can you establish a stable connection?”

Samar worked quickly, adjusting frequencies. “Done. We can respond in real-time.”

RoHit rubbed his hands together. “Ooooh, can I send the first message? Something classy, like ‘What’s up, space dudes?’”

Aditi groaned. “Absolutely not.”

The screen flickered as the translated response was sent:

“Who are you? Why were you waiting?”

For a moment, there was silence. Then, new symbols appeared, rapidly translating:

“You are the first to arrive. We prepared. We observed. We hoped.”

RoHit leaned in. “Oh, great, they sound like my parents when I finally visit home.”

Aditi shot him a look. “Not the time.”

Vikram kept his eyes on the data stream. “What exactly did they prepare for?”

The response came almost instantly.

“To share knowledge. To ensure survival.”

Aditi blinked. “Survival? That’s… unsettling.”

RoHit scratched his chin. “Or they’re just really bad at phrasing things. Like me.”

Arjun frowned at his console. “I don’t like this. What are they surviving from?”

Samar’s screen suddenly lit up with a complex diagram. The alien entity was sending them coordinates… to something nearby.

Vikram studied it. “This is close. A structure - another station?”

Aditi sighed. “I have a bad feeling about this.”

RoHit clapped his hands. “Which is exactly why we should check it out! What’s life without a little danger, right?”

Aditi rolled her eyes. “You’re impossible.”

RoHit grinned. “And yet, you’d all be so bored without me.”

As Vyom-1 neared the coordinates, a massive, ancient structure came into view. It was unlike anything they had ever seen - part mechanical, part organic, its surface pulsating faintly with energy.

Arjun’s eyes widened. “That… is definitely not natural.”

Aditi exhaled. “And we’re just flying toward it, no questions asked?”

RoHit put a reassuring hand on her shoulder. “Relax, Aditi. What’s the worst that could-”

A sudden jolt rocked the ship. The structure had emitted an energy pulse, causing Vyom-1’s systems to flicker.

Aditi glared at him. “WHY DO YOU ALWAYS SAY THAT?!”

RoHit winced. “Okay, fair point.”

Samar checked the readings. “We’re fine. But I think… we just activated something.”

Vikram nodded. “We need to investigate. Let’s suit up.”

The team boarded the lander and approached the structure, docking at an opening that seemed made for them. As they entered, they found themselves in a vast chamber, filled with intricate designs and floating holographic displays.

Aditi scanned the symbols. “It’s a database… of knowledge.”

RoHit grinned. “A space library? Sweet! Do they have comic books?”

Aditi ignored him. “If this contains advanced technology or warnings about dangers, it could change everything.”

Suddenly, the chamber pulsed with light. The message system activated again.

“The test begins. Prove yourselves.”

Arjun tensed. “Test? What test?”

RoHit held up his hands. “Okay, before anyone blames me, this one was NOT my fault.”

The doors sealed behind them.

Aditi sighed. “I hate you, RoHit.”

RoHit winked. “You’d miss me if I weren’t here.”

The crew braced themselves for what was to come.
