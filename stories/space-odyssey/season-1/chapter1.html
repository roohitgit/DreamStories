Chapter 1: The Final Selection

The year was 2054. India stood on the brink of a new era preparing for the first crewed journey beyond the Solar System.

After three successful unmanned missions to Alpha Centauri, the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) had been given the honor of leading humanity’s first interstellar voyage.

The mission was ambitious, risky, and historic.

RoHit sat in the waiting hall of the Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre, his fingers tapping lightly on his knee.

The final crew selection was moments away. Unlike the other candidates, RoHit wasn’t a specialist. He wasn’t the best scientist, engineer, or pilot.

But he was an all-rounder capable of handling multiple disciplines at a functional level. In training, he had proved his adaptability, but against a team of experts, he often felt like an outlier.

Would the committee see his versatility as an asset or a flaw?

The heavy doors slid open, and the selection committee walked in. The room fell silent as Commander Rakesh Menon, the head of the interstellar mission program, stepped forward.

"Today, we choose the six who will make history," he announced.

"You will be the first humans to travel to Alpha Centauri. This mission will test the limits of human endurance, intellect, and cooperation. The world will watch as you represent not just India, but all of humanity."

A hush settled over the room. Then, one by one, the names were called.

• Dr. Aditi Rao - astrophysicist and mission scientist.

• Commander Vikram Patel - ace pilot and navigation expert.

• Dr. Ananya Iyer - medical officer and biologist.

• Arjun Prasad - chief engineer and systems specialist.

• Samar Mehta - communications and AI expert.

• RoHit Sharma - mission support and operations.

RoHit’s breath caught. Had he misheard? His name was among the chosen. A mixture of excitement and disbelief surged through him.

He was going to Alpha Centauri.

As he glanced around, he noticed the varied reactions. Some of the selected astronauts exchanged smiles, while others assessed their new teammates. He could sense the unspoken question in some of their eyes: Why him?

Commander Menon, as if anticipating the doubt, spoke again.

"Each of you has a role that is critical to this mission. RoHit, your ability to adapt, to step into any role as needed, makes you invaluable. This crew will need flexibility as much as expertise."

A murmur of understanding passed through the room. RoHit exhaled, straightening his posture. This was real. This was happening.

As the team was led into the next briefing, the magnitude of their journey began to sink in. They would be traveling at near-light speed, stepping into the unknown. There would be no turning back. The challenges ahead would be unlike anything faced before.

But for RoHit, this was more than a mission. It was his chance to prove that adaptability was not a weakness - it was a strength.

The first step toward the stars had been taken.
