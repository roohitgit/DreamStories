Chapter 3: The First Challenge

As Vyom-1 journeyed deeper into interstellar space, the vast emptiness of the cosmos began to test the crew’s endurance. Time slowed, and the reality of their distance from Earth sank in. But just as monotony threatened to settle, the ship’s emergency alarms blared to life.

A sudden burst of static filled the communication channels, followed by Samar Mehta’s urgent voice. “Commander, we have a situation. An unknown anomaly is interfering with our navigation systems.”

RoHit, who had been floating lazily near the observation deck, flipped himself upright. “Are we being haunted by space ghosts? Because I did not sign up for that.”

Dr. Aditi Rao smirked. “Unlikely, RoHit. But I do appreciate your dedication to humor.”

Commander Vikram Patel quickly analyzed the readings. “Samar, what kind of anomaly?”

Samar's fingers flew across the console. “It’s a gravitational distortion, about 50,000 kilometers ahead. It’s pulling us slightly off course.”

Arjun Prasad, the chief engineer, frowned. “If we don’t adjust, we could end up wasting fuel correcting our trajectory later.”

RoHit rubbed his chin dramatically. “So, what you’re saying is, we either do some fancy space maneuvering or risk running out of gas? Great. I vote for the fancy maneuver.”

Vikram nodded. “Agreed. Samar, plot a course correction. Arjun, monitor the ship’s engines. RoHit, keep an eye on any unexpected fluctuations.”

As the team worked together, RoHit’s role became crucial. While not an expert, his ability to assist in multiple disciplines allowed the specialists to focus on their core tasks. The ship successfully navigated around the gravitational distortion, avoiding any major setbacks.

After the crisis was averted, RoHit stretched. “Well, that was fun. Let’s not do it again.”

The crew laughed, tension easing. Though the journey had just begun, Vyom-1 had already faced its first challenge, and RoHit had proven that even in critical moments, a little humor went a long way.
