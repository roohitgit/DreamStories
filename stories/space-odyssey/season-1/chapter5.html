Chapter 5: The Great Spacewalk Mishap

The crew of Vyom-1 had barely caught their breath from the reactor crisis when another challenge emerged - this time, outside the ship.

“Commander, we have a structural anomaly on the exterior hull,” Samar reported, his eyes scanning the data. “It looks like a micrometeoroid strike.”

Vikram Patel sighed. “Damage report?”

Aditi Rao pulled up the visuals from the ship’s external cameras. “Nothing catastrophic, but a small breach in the outer thermal shielding. If we don’t fix it soon, we risk deeper structural damage as we accelerate.”

Arjun Prasad was already calculating solutions. “We need a spacewalk to patch the breach manually. I can suit up and-”

RoHit cut in. “Hold on, hold on! You guys are forgetting something important.”

Ananya raised an eyebrow. “That you’re the best at fixing things?”

RoHit grinned. “No, that I look much cooler in a spacesuit than Arjun. And let’s be honest, space needs a hero.”

Vikram smirked. “Alright, hero. But be careful out there.”

Minutes later, RoHit stood in the airlock, suited up and tethered to the ship. As the outer doors opened, the vastness of space greeted him. He let out a dramatic sigh. “So this is what it feels like to be a Bollywood star in a sci-fi movie.”

Arjun’s voice crackled in his earpiece. “Less drama, more patching, please.”

RoHit drifted toward the damaged panel, his every movement slow and calculated. He pulled out the repair kit and assessed the breach.

“Alright, step one: don’t float away into oblivion. Step two: try not to poke a hole in the ship.”

He began applying the sealant, humming to himself as he worked. Everything was going smoothly - until the ship shuddered unexpectedly.

“Uh, guys?” RoHit’s voice turned serious. “Did someone just hit the emergency eject button, or is the ship moving?”

Samar’s voice came through. “We’re experiencing a minor course correction. Hold tight.”

RoHit huffed. “Holding tight is my specialty.”

The shift in momentum made maneuvering trickier, but RoHit stayed focused. After a few more adjustments, he sealed the breach. “Mission accomplished!”

As he turned to head back, he noticed something unusual - a faint glow on the edge of the ship.

“Uh, guys? Either I’m seeing things, or there’s some weird light reflecting off the hull.”

Aditi quickly pulled up the external sensors. “RoHit, describe what you’re seeing.”

“It’s like… a faint bluish shimmer. Almost like a distortion in space.”

Vikram’s tone sharpened. “Get back inside. Now.”

RoHit, sensing the urgency, didn’t argue. He propelled himself toward the airlock. As he reached it, the shimmering light flickered - and then disappeared.

He entered the airlock and the doors sealed behind him. The crew greeted him with a mix of relief and curiosity.

Arjun patted his shoulder. “Well, hero, did you bring back any space souvenirs?”

RoHit smirked. “Just an unexplained mystery. You know, the usual.”

The incident left them with more questions than answers, but for now, Vyom-1 was secure, and RoHit had once again saved the day - with style.

Little did they know, the strange glow was only the beginning of something far greater waiting ahead.
