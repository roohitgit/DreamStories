Chapter 11: The Unseen Depths

The golden twin suns of Prithvi-2 bathed the rocky terrain in an eerie glow as the crew of Vyom-1 continued their mission on the alien world. Their initial landing was behind them, but the true challenge had just begun. They had a planet to explore - one filled with unknown elements, potential dangers, and perhaps, long-buried secrets waiting to be uncovered.

RoHit, as usual, was the first to break the tense atmosphere. He adjusted his helmet and grinned at Aditi. “So, do I get to name something this time? I was thinking RoHit Crater has a nice ring to it.”

Aditi rolled her eyes. “You mean the one you accidentally made by dropping a supply crate?”

“Details, details,” RoHit said, waving off the comment. “Point is, I’m leaving my mark on history.”

Commander Vikram Patel cleared his throat, bringing everyone back to focus. “Alright, enough joking around. We need to set up a long-term base, secure water sources, and begin mapping the surrounding region. We’ll split into teams. Ananya and Samar will work on scanning for subterranean water. RoHit, Aditi, and Arjun, you’ll investigate the unusual energy readings coming from the northern ridge.”

“Wait, wait, wait,” RoHit interjected. “You’re sending me into the unknown? Have you learned nothing from horror movies?”

Aditi smirked. “Relax, hero. If anything scary jumps out, you can use your bad jokes as a defense mechanism.”

The Journey to the Ridge.

The trio made their way across the uneven terrain, their exo-suits adjusting to the planet’s slightly lower gravity. Jagged rock formations jutted out at odd angles, and strange bioluminescent plants pulsed with a faint blue light, casting ghostly shadows around them.

RoHit tapped one of the glowing plants with his gloved finger. “Either this thing is friendly, or I just initiated an alien invasion.”

Arjun scanned the plants. “They appear to react to touch, but no signs of toxicity. Fascinating.”

As they climbed the northern ridge, their sensors picked up an unusual energy signature. It was pulsing - almost rhythmic.

Aditi checked her readings. “This is definitely artificial. Whatever’s here… it wasn’t made by nature.”

RoHit’s bravado faltered slightly. “So, on a scale of ‘cool discovery’ to ‘run for your lives,’ where do we stand?”

Arjun hesitated before answering. “Too early to tell.”

The Discovery.

Reaching the source of the signal, they found themselves standing before a massive chasm. At the bottom, partially buried in sediment, was what appeared to be a structure. A massive, hexagonal doorway was embedded into the rock wall, pulsing faintly with an amber light.

Aditi took a deep breath. “That… is not natural.”

RoHit leaned in. “It also isn’t open. Looks like someone forgot to leave the key under the mat.”

Arjun was already working on his scanner. “There’s a control panel on the right side. Looks ancient but… intact.”

RoHit couldn’t resist. “Well, I am an expert in ancient alien technology. Step aside.”

Aditi grabbed his arm. “You’re an expert in breaking things. Let Arjun handle it.”

Arjun carefully analyzed the panel. It had no obvious buttons - just a smooth surface with symbols etched into it. “These symbols… they look like they follow a sequence. If we can decipher the pattern, we might be able to access the structure.”

Breaking In - The RoHit Way.

RoHit crouched down, rubbing his chin. “Or… hear me out. We press all the buttons and see what happens.”

Aditi groaned. “That is literally the worst idea.”

But before anyone could stop him, RoHit reached out and tapped a random symbol. The panel lit up, and the ground beneath them trembled.

Arjun paled. “RoHit, what did you do?!”

Before he could answer, the massive doors creaked open, revealing a spiraling staircase descending into the darkness.

RoHit grinned. “See? I told you I’m an expert.”

Aditi smacked his arm. “You got lucky! What if it triggered a defense mechanism?”

RoHit shrugged. “Then I’ll just joke my way out of it.”

Into the Unknown.

The team exchanged glances before stepping inside. The moment they crossed the threshold, their suits’ lights flickered briefly, as if the energy within the structure interfered with their systems.

The walls were lined with intricate carvings, depicting what seemed to be an ancient civilization - tall, humanoid beings with elongated limbs, working with advanced technology.

Arjun whispered in awe, “This could be the remnants of an intelligent species… and it’s been here for centuries, maybe longer.”

RoHit smirked. “Great, we just broke into an alien museum. Hope they don’t charge us an entrance fee.”

As they moved deeper, a faint hum echoed through the corridor. The light pulsations along the walls intensified, and suddenly, a holographic figure materialized before them.

Aditi’s breath hitched. “Is that… an AI?”

The figure, humanoid in shape but featureless, spoke in a deep, resonant tone. “You are not of this world. Why have you come?”

RoHit, without missing a beat, saluted dramatically. “We come in peace! And also with snacks. Do you guys have chips here?”

The AI paused. “Your presence was foretold. The Last Guardians left warnings.”

Arjun stepped forward. “Warnings about what?”

The hologram flickered. “The planet was not abandoned. Something still remains below.”

The ground rumbled again, this time more violently.

RoHit looked around nervously. “Uh… I hate to be that guy, but I think we just woke something up.”

As dust cascaded from the ceiling, the mission had taken a dramatic turn. What had once seemed like an empty planet now held a dark, buried secret.

And they were about to uncover it - whether they were ready or not.
