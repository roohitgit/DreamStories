Chapter 6: The Gravity Crisis

The crew of Vyom-1 barely had time to process the strange light RoHit had witnessed outside the ship when a new problem arose - one that threatened their entire mission.

Arjun Prasad’s voice was urgent. “Commander, we have a gravity fluctuation. Artificial gravity is destabilizing.”

Vikram Patel’s eyes narrowed. “What’s causing it?”

Samar Mehta was already running diagnostics. “It’s the inertial dampeners. They’re miscalculating our acceleration rate. If this continues, we could be thrown into a gravitational anomaly.”

RoHit, hanging upside down from a seatbelt he had hastily strapped himself into, waved his arms. “Okay, so let’s fix it before we all become floating samosas.”

Ananya Iyer tried stabilizing herself. “RoHit, this is serious!”

“I know! But would you rather freak out or laugh while we fix it?”

Vikram nodded. “He’s right. We need to act fast. Arjun, what do we do?”

Arjun analyzed the readings. “We need to manually reset the dampeners. But the control panel is in the lower deck.”

RoHit unbuckled himself, drifting in midair. “And let me guess… someone has to float down there and fix it.”

Aditi smirked. “We volunteer you.”

RoHit sighed dramatically. “Fine, but if I die, name a space station after me.”

Drifting through the corridor was trickier than expected. The artificial gravity flickered on and off, making movement unpredictable. RoHit grumbled as he dodged a rogue toolbox floating past his head.

Reaching the lower deck, he saw the problem - the gravity control module was flickering erratically, its circuits overloaded.

“Alright, I’ve seen enough sci-fi movies to know that hitting it might work,” RoHit joked, tapping the panel.

Nothing happened.

“Plan B,” he muttered. He accessed the manual override, fingers flying over the controls. The screen flashed warnings in red.

“Okay, okay… If I reverse the polarity - no, that’s Star Trek logic… Maybe if I bypass the overload…”

His comm buzzed. “RoHit, status?” Vikram’s voice was tense.

“Working on it, Commander. Just having a romantic moment with the control panel.”

Suddenly, the ship jolted, and gravity surged back - too strong.

RoHit was slammed onto the floor. “Ow! Okay, that worked… but too much!”

“Reduce it by 30%!” Arjun instructed.

RoHit, still flat on the floor, groaned. “I’d love to, but my bones are now part of the floor.”

Summoning his strength, he reached up and adjusted the controls. Slowly, the gravity stabilized.

Aditi’s voice came through. “We’re back to normal! Nice work, RoHit!”

RoHit pushed himself up, stretching. “No applause? No medals? Not even a free chai?”

Ananya chuckled. “How about dinner duty for the next week?”

RoHit sighed. “This is why heroes never get paid enough.”

As he made his way back to the crew, he couldn’t shake the feeling that these constant malfunctions weren’t just bad luck. Something was interfering with Vyom-1. And they needed to find out what - before it was too late.
